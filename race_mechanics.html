System Notes

Race Mechanics: Trick-Taking Identities

These are playtest mechanics for the six peoples of the hollow using the existing trick-taking engine. Most of these are now concrete rules rather than loose identity notes, with each race bending a different part of the card flow, recovery cycle, or valid-play structure.

Design Frame

The base system already has strong levers: forced follow, trump by challenge suit, a fixed seven-card hand, volatile status cards, and consolation effects on trick loss. These race rules work best when they stay short, sharp, and visibly tied to those levers.

  • Most races should have one signature exception or substitution, not a whole sub-engine.
  • Status pressure, shuffling, and check-specific draws are the cleanest knobs for race identity here.
  • Any race rule should still fit on a player aid or a single card.

Varn

Binding Bargain Hand Reset

The Varn mechanic should feel like a dangerous contract made under pressure: when their current hand will not do, they can bargain for a new one and accept that the price will come due later.

  • Rule: A Varn may propose a curse to the GM in order to discard their hand to draw another.
  • Cost: The curse should be specific, active, and meaningful enough to matter in later play rather than being a cosmetic drawback.
  • Play identity: Varn trade a bad present hand for future trouble, which matches their place as people who make binding bargains and live inside the consequences.

Moon-Eyed

Wax Resilience Challenge Interference

The Moon-Eyed should feel hard to kill and subtly disruptive, as if the little candleflames behind their eyes can bend the shape of misfortune without overpowering the whole game.

  • Rule: When shuffling, a Moon-Eyed may remove one additional Injury.
  • Rule: Once per check, a Moon-Eyed may discard 1 card from their hand and draw 1 card from their own deck.
  • Play identity: They endure bodily damage better than most and can rework their hand through flickering, candleflame intuition.

Tarrans

Ichor Tolerance Weird Handling

Tarrans need a rule that makes Weird cards feel different in their hands than in anyone else's. Since they can safely drink raw ichor, Weird should become a more reliable tool for them, especially when the current check makes the led suit trump.

  • Rule: Tarrans must treat Weird as the led suit for the purposes of valid play.
  • Effect: This is especially strong when the led suit is also trump, because a Tarran using Weird can satisfy follow-suit pressure with the trump suit itself.
  • Play identity: Tarrans handle dangerous magic so comfortably that Weird becomes a dependable road for them rather than a merely strange one.

Hollow-Skinned

Feeding Curse Predatory Surge

The Hollow-Skinned mechanic works best if repeated injury pushes them toward a dangerous feeding state. Once they cross that line, they become stronger in direct violence, carry a curse that demands action, and turn impersonation into a way of stealing usable strengths.

  • Rule: After taking an Injury with an injury in the discard, a Hollow-Skinned gains a specific Curse representing the need to consume.
  • While active: They may draw 2 extra cards on Strength checks.
  • Borrowed advantage: While not in primal form, they also gain the racial advantage of the person they are currently inhabiting.
  • Play identity: Accumulated harm pushes them toward monstrous appetite, and feeding lets them become a more dangerous and opportunistic version of whoever they have stolen.

Rootborn

Rebalancing Growth Deck Ecology

The Rootborn mechanic should feel like restoring balance instead of simply clearing harm. Their deck wants to regrow toward whatever suit has become sparse.

  • Rule: When shuffling, a Rootborn may choose not to remove a status card.
  • If they do: Add 1 card of the suit with the fewest cards currently in their deck.
  • Play identity: Rootborn recover by restoring imbalance in the living pattern of the deck rather than just scraping harm away.

Ashcrik

Two Forms Fasting Cycle

The Ashcrik rule is now a clear long-form cycle: they choose a body state for the current stretch of play, and switching is paid for through repeated fasting harm across two shuffles rather than flipping freely from moment to moment.

  • Reed-form: Draw an extra card on Dexterity checks.
  • Ember-form: Draw an extra card on Strength checks.
  • Switching: Declare a fast and carry it across 2 shuffles. Gain 1 Dazed on each of those shuffles. At the end of the second shuffle, switch forms. Interrupting a fast cancels the form switch and adds a stress.

Current Playtest Spread

The current race identities now cluster around a few clean subsystems:

  • Varn: exchange a bad hand for a new one by taking on a negotiated curse.
  • Moon-Eyed: better Injury recovery and hand-cycling.
  • Tarrans: Weird cards always count as the led suit for them.
  • Hollow-Skinned: Injury triggers a hunger curse, a stronger Strength surge, and borrowed racial advantage.
  • Rootborn: reshuffle growth instead of status removal.
  • Ashcrik: form-specific extra draws gated by a two-shuffle fast.

This is a strong spread because each people touches a different part of the engine: hand reset, hand-cycling, Weird handling, curse pressure, reshuffle tuning, and long-cycle stance switching.
