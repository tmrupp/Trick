Trick-Taking RPG: Player Quick Reference[cite: 1]

1. Core Rules

  • Hand Size: 7 cards.[cite: 1]
  • Play: Highest trump card wins the trick, or highest card of any suit if no trump is played.[cite: 1]
  • Trump Suit: Determined by the suit of the current challenge/check.[cite: 1]
  • The Open Hand (Opposition): Always plays the leftmost valid card and must follow the led suit.[cite: 1]

2. Suits & Status Cards

Suit / Check Status Card (0 Value) Effects & Removal
Strength (Physical)[cite: 1] Injury[cite: 1] Death instantly if 4th drawn into hand.[cite: 1] 2 in hand at start triggers Survival Event.[cite: 1] Remove via Rest or Reshuffle.[cite: 1]
Dexterity (Agility)[cite: 1] Dazed[cite: 1] Must be played if it is a valid play.[cite: 1] Permanently removed from game upon play.[cite: 1] Also removed via Rest or Reshuffle.[cite: 1]
Intelligence (Knowledge)[cite: 1] Stress[cite: 1] Duplicates an identical copy to discard pile when drawn.[cite: 1] Remove via Rest or Reshuffle.[cite: 1]
Weird (Magic/Unknown)[cite: 1] Curse[cite: 1] Carries a GM-written compulsion.[cite: 1] Only removed by fulfilling the GM-set condition (cannot be rested away).[cite: 1]

3. Trick Loss Consolations (Optional)

Triggers when losing a single trick. The effect is based on the suit you played, regardless of what beat you.[cite: 1]

  • Strength (Brace): Stage your played card outside your hand; add its full value to your next play.[cite: 1]
  • Dexterity (Reposition): Choose which valid card the open hand must play next trick.[cite: 1]
  • Intelligence (Recalculate): Discard any 1 card from your hand, then draw 1 from your deck.[cite: 1]
  • Weird (Revelation): Ask the GM one question, which must be answered honestly.[cite: 1]

4. Recovery

  • Rest Scene (GM Discretion): Remove any number of status cards from your deck (except Curses).[cite: 1]
  • Reshuffle (Deck Empty): Remove exactly 1 eligible status card from your discard pile before shuffling (except Curses).[cite: 1]
