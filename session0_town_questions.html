Session 0 Guide

Questions for Building the Town and Townsfolk

Use these in session 0 to establish the players' home town together. You do not need all of them. Pick the questions that produce useful specifics: a landmark, a household, a local feud, a custom, a pact, a rumor, or a person everyone will remember.

How to Use These

  • Ask 1 or 2 questions from each section rather than trying to clear the whole list.
  • Whenever a player answers in broad terms, follow with: "What is one concrete example?"
  • Write down names immediately: creeks, families, churches, stores, hills, spirits, and the dead all matter here.
  • End each section by asking who benefits, who suffers, and what everyone knows not to say aloud.

First Shape of the Town

Scale
  1. What kind of place is this: a crossroads town, a creek settlement, a mining hollow, a rail stop, a church town, a market town, or something else?
  2. What is the first thing a stranger notices when they come in?
  3. What is the one building or landmark everyone uses to give directions?
  4. What part of town feels oldest, and what story do people tell about why it is still standing?
Daily Life
  1. What does this town live on materially: orchards, livestock, timber, ferries, coal, stilling, milling, trade, pilgrimage, or something stranger?
  2. What seasonal work reshapes everybody's schedule?
  3. What does the town have too little of every year?
  4. What ordinary sound defines the place at dusk?

Places That Matter

Landmarks
  1. What place in town is considered safe, and why do people trust it?
  2. What place is useful but quietly feared?
  3. Where do young people go when they want privacy, trouble, or both?
  4. What nearby natural feature has a proper name and a bad reputation?
  5. Which road, bridge, ford, cemetery, or orchard edge is known to be haunted, watched, or claimed?
  6. What place has changed owners too many times, and what does everyone think is wrong with it?

Power, Families, and Reputation

Influence
  1. Who is the most respected person in town, and what have they done to earn that respect?
  2. Who has power without being liked?
  3. What family has been here long enough to think the town belongs to them?
  4. Who keeps the books, the keys, or the names, and how do they use that leverage?
Reputation
  1. What kind of mistake will get remembered for twenty years in this town?
  2. What scandal does everyone know but nobody states plainly?
  3. Who is spoken of as a fool but is secretly right about something important?
  4. Who left town and became a warning story?

Pacts, Spirits, and Taboos

Supernatural Life
  1. What kind of supernatural being is most entangled with the town: creek things, mine ghosts, orchard saints, house spirits, weather powers, road-haunters, or something else?
  2. What is the most common small pact ordinary people make?
  3. What local taboo does everyone obey even if they pretend not to believe in it?
  4. What offering is left at a specific place, and what happens if it is neglected?
  5. What kind of bargain is considered shameful even if it works?
  6. Who in town is known to have a dangerous patron, and why are they still tolerated?

The Six Peoples in Town

Belonging
  1. Which people are most numerous here, and which are rare?
  2. Which people are treated as "from here" no matter what, and which are still treated as outsiders?
  3. Which people are trusted with dangerous work, and which are trusted with delicate work?
  4. What old insult or old compliment does the town still use for one of the peoples?
Race-Specific Prompts
  1. What bargain or contract in town is famous because a Varn witnessed it?
  2. What do the Moon-Eyed do better than anyone else after dark?
  3. What do people rely on Tarrans to bring down from the ridges?
  4. What law, ritual, or custom exists because the Hollow-Skinned are real?
  5. What place can only be understood properly with Rootborn help?
  6. What job or ritual absolutely depends on Ashcrik switching forms at the right time?

Town Conflict

Current Trouble
  1. What problem is getting worse right now?
  2. Who is profiting from that problem?
  3. What practical thing does the town need before winter, before market day, or before the river rises?
  4. What feud or grievance is one bad week away from turning violent?
Hidden Trouble
  1. What has recently gone missing that people are trying not to panic about?
  2. What place has started behaving wrong?
  3. Who made a pact they should not have made?
  4. What truth about the town's founding would hurt living people if it came out?

Personal Ties

Player Hooks
  1. Which townsfolk does each player character owe something to?
  2. Who in town thinks one of the player characters is still a child?
  3. Who expects one of the characters to take over a job, debt, or responsibility they do not want?
  4. What local place mattered deeply to one of the characters when they were younger?
  5. Which townsfolk would hide a character from the law, and which would not?
  6. What event made the whole player group known to the town before play even begins?

Fast Finishers

If you want to end session 0 with a town that already feels dense and playable, make sure you leave with answers to these ten questions:

  1. What is the town called?
  2. What does it live on?
  3. What place is feared?
  4. Who is powerful?
  5. What pact is common?
  6. What taboo is real?
  7. What trouble is immediate?
  8. What race-specific custom shapes daily life?
  9. Who do the player characters already know?
  10. What secret would change how the town sees itself?
